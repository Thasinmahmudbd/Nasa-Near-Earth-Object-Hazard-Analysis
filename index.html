Context

Near-Earth objects are asteroids and comets with orbits that bring them to within 120 million miles of the Sun, which means they can circulate through the Earth's orbital neighborhood. This analysis is about foreign object hazard probability.

Data Cleaning:

Since this dataset was pulled from Kaggle to much cleaning was not necessary. I trimmed few unnecessary data & added a few columns by using the existing data for analysis. I also calculated the respective Ranges of Absolute Magnitude provided.

Dashboard:

This is an interactive Visualization/Dashboard where you can see a report of potential hazardous asteroids, Sentry object count & also a comparison of both to see the amount of sentry objects that are prone to become hazardous.


Filtering is possible based on asteroid Orbit classes,

  • AMO - Stands for Amor. Near-Earth asteroid orbits similar to that of 1221 Amor (a > 1.0 AU; 1.017 AU < q < 1.3 AU).
  • APO - Stands for Apollo. Near-Earth asteroid orbits which cross the Earth's orbit similar to that of 1862 Apollo (a > 1.0 AU; q < 1.017 AU).
  • ATE - Stands for Aten. Near-Earth asteroid orbits similar to that of 2062 Aten (a < 1.0 AU; Q > 0.983 AU).
  • IEO - Stands for Interior Earth Object. An asteroid orbit contained entirely within the orbit of the Earth (Q < 0.983 AU).

    • Further filtering is possible by cancelling out the possibility of an object being sentry or by only analyzing the hazardous asteroids.
